5th Annual NYUSFF Award Winners Announced!

The NYU Sports Film Festival is pleased to announce the jury and audience award winners from its 5th Annual edition, celebrating exceptional storytelling in sports cinema from around the world.

2025 Award Winners

After careful deliberation by our esteemed jury panel and audience voting, we're proud to announce the winners of the 5th Annual NYU Sports Film Festival. This year's selections represent the best in sports filmmaking, showcasing diverse stories that capture the human spirit through athletics.

Best Documentary Feature

Winner: "Kingdome"
This powerful documentary chronicles Harlem's legendary Kingdome basketball tournament, exploring its legacy of resilience, community, and the unifying power of the game.

Best Narrative Short

Winner: "The Eight Count"
A compelling story of a Korean-American boxer who fights to protect her younger brother, blending the struggles of family survival with the grit of the boxing ring.

Best International Film

Winner: "Marie's Ambition"
A fascinating tale of a French chess player tempted by a mysterious official offering supernatural assistance to improve her game.

Best Cinematography

Winner: "Sand Dancers"
Stunning visuals capture quadriplegic surfer Jimmy Simpson's journey as he defies the odds with support from Britain's northeast coast community.

Audience Choice Award

Winner: "The First World Basketball Day"
This inspiring film follows Professor David Hollander's journey to establish World Basketball Day, celebrating basketball's global power to unite and inspire.

Student Film Award

Winner: "Ān-dé-lǔ"
A sensitive portrayal of thirteen-year-old Andrew Chong navigating life on his baseball team while dealing with cultural differences and family pressures.

Best Emerging Filmmaker

Winner: "No Mistakes"
An innovative film where a volleyball player confronts his own subconscious to redeem a game-ending mistake.

Special Recognition Awards

"Without Glory" - Special Recognition for Performance
Adrian Davis's portrayal of a 78-year-old boxing trainer balancing caregiving duties with his passion for the sport.

"Tough Moves" - Special Recognition for Direction
Exceptional direction in chronicling thirteen-year-old Arminius juggling chess and boxing under his father's guidance.

"Surrender to the Sound" - Special Recognition for Community Impact
Celebrating Angel City FC's supporter groups and their role in redefining women's soccer fandom.

"On the Hill" - Special Recognition for Cultural Preservation
A thoughtful portrait of young ski jumpers and the communities keeping the sport alive in America.

"The Call of Combat" - Special Recognition for Sports Diversity
Highlighting four distinct combat disciplines and the values that unite their communities.

About the Jury

This year's jury panel included distinguished filmmakers, sports industry professionals, and media experts who evaluated films based on storytelling excellence, technical achievement, and cultural impact. The audience choice award was determined by festival attendees who voted after each screening.

The 5th Annual NYU Sports Film Festival received submissions from over 20 countries, demonstrating the global appeal and universal themes found in sports storytelling.

Congratulations to all our winners and participants. These films represent the future of sports cinema and the power of storytelling to inspire, unite, and celebrate the human spirit through athletics.
