My Father, HSV, and the Nazis - Football under Fascists (29:25)
Directed by: Katrin Hafemann and Marie Teresa Lasserre
Country of Origin: Germany

Synopsis: After his father’s death, Swedish man Torkel Wachter finds documents from Nazi Germany in a cardboard box. They concern a man named Walter who played football for HSV and was later sent to a concentration camp. They reveal his father’s true identity, which Torkel is only able to piece together after years of research; research that brings Hamburg football’s dark history to life - from the 1920s to matches among prisoners at the Neuengamme concentration camp, where former HSV star Tull Harder worked as a guard for the SS.

Windward (5:53)
Directed by: Luna Barreiro, Renn Smith, Flynn Rossington, and Alex Powell
Country of Origin: United States (California)


Synopsis: After years of living with an inherited retinal disease, Walt Raineri believed he had prepared for blindness. But he soon realized it would not be that simple. Following his journey within a community of blind sailors, this film invites us into an immersive sensory world where navigation becomes listening, feeling, and trusting the boat, wind, and one another. He uses the water to feel comfort, freedom, and to challenge himself, and his story opens into a broader exploration of what it means to be human, moving from one moment of ability to the next.
