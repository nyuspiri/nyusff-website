Jerome “Junkyard Dog (JYD)” Williams
Jerome Williams is a Georgetown University graduate, renowned athlete, television producer, head coach and popular businessman with an impeccable reputation and mega heart. He is known for his professionalism and the willingness to readily give back to those less fortunate. 

Bryan Terry
Bryan Terry is a veteran leader in the sports and entertainment media landscape, currently serving as the head of the Players Studio at PlayersTV. With a career defined by innovative storytelling, Bryan has a proven track record of developing high-impact show formats and digital series that bridge the gap between world-class athletes and their audiences. His extensive production background stems from tenures at industry powerhouses including MTV, truTV, and Vice, where he honed his expertise in navigating the complexities of modern, talent-focused media. Currently, he is overseeing several major documentary projects, including a highly anticipated film centered on Penn State. Beyond the studio, Bryan serves as a Board Member for The Picture House Regional Film Center, where he contributes to the strategic growth of independent cinema. A resident of Pelham, New York, his passion for the medium extends to a deep appreciation for the intersection of sports, culture, and community.

Aron Phillips
Aron Phillips is a media and content executive operating at the intersection of sports, culture, and storytelling. He is the founder of How To Make It, a long-running platform focused on creative development and intellectual property, and currently serves as a Chief Content Consultant at Same Page Entertainment and a founding member of The Nonfiction Hotlist, a collective dedicated to premium unscripted storytelling.

Phillips previously served as Chief Executive Officer of RTG Features—the film and television studio behind SLAM’s expansion into long-form storytelling—and as Chief Operating Officer of both RTG Features and SLAM, the iconic basketball media brand turned global lifestyle platform. In these roles, he led strategic growth, original content development, and the adaptation of SLAM’s extensive archive into scripted and unscripted projects across film, television, and digital.

Before SLAM, Phillips was a Senior Director and Director of Development at UNINTERRUPTED and a Creative Executive at SpringHill Entertainment, the athlete-driven media company founded by LeBron James and Maverick Carter. His work across these platforms includes producing acclaimed projects such as The Carter Effect, Gap Year, and the Sports Emmy–winning series Greatness Code.
