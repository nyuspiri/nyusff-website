2026 NYU Sports Film Festival Overview
The 6th Annual NYU Sports Film Festival (April 17–19, 2026) delivered its most expansive and well-attended program to date, bringing together industry leaders, student filmmakers, and international guests for three days of screenings, panels, and live events.

Pitch Contest (Opening Event)
The festival opened with its flagship Pitch Contest, featuring a panel of high-level industry judges:
Jerome Williams — Former NBA player, Chairman & CEO of IP FAMBA Inc.
Aron Phillips — Chief Content Consultant at Same Page Entertainment, former COO of SLAM
Bryan Terry — President of Players Studio, PlayersTV
The event drew a strong audience and included both pre-selected finalists and a live “wild card” round with audience participation.
Winner:
Nic Chang — Jürgen Locadia
 A feature documentary in development on Curaçao international footballer Jürgen Locadia, currently playing in the USL in Miami.

Opening Night
Opening Night featured two major screenings and one of the highest-attended events of the weekend:
Unraveling George (Dir. Mike Tollin)
Attended by notable basketball figures including:
Walt Frazier
Bill Bradley
William Wesley
Open Book (Fresh Focus Sports)
A powerful comeback story centered on former coach Emmanuel “Book” Richardson
Followed by a highly emotional Q&A with Richardson and filmmakers Thanasis Petrakis and Anthony Kuzviwanza
The evening concluded with a packed networking reception, reflecting strong turnout and engagement across attendees.

Industry Programming: Gersh U Panels
Saturday programming featured Gersh U, a series of panels with leading voices across sports, media, and culture.
Key participants included:
Henry Gaskins — Head of Think450
Deirunas Visockas — EVP, Gersh Sports, NBPA-certified agent
Jared Thompson — VP, Alternative Programming
Schuyler Roos
Caleb Miner — Worked with PUMA, NASCAR, Chris Paul, Carmelo Anthony
Ionie Banner — Focused on data, equity, and philanthropy
Moderators included:
Jayson Council — Head of Culture, Gersh
Julia Benavides
Raven Vaz
These panels drew strong attendance and highlighted the festival’s growing role as a bridge between sports and entertainment industries.

Film Programming
Across the weekend, the festival showcased a diverse slate of films:
Night 2: 6 short films + filmmaker Q&As
Night 3: 7 short films + filmmaker Q&As
In addition, the festival featured the Sports Documentary Course Screening, highlighting work from students in NYU’s pioneering course led by Ronnie Kay.

Awards & Recognition
The festival concluded with a well-attended Awards Ceremony, distributing over $4,000 in prizes.
Major Award Winners:
Best NYU Documentary — Hold On (Will Fitzpatrick) — $300
Best Open Documentary — Shifting Courts (David Fernández Graña) — $300
Best NYU Narrative — Bleeding Edge (Daeyoung Kim) — $300
Best Open Narrative — Showtime (Francesco Giardiello) — $300
Audience Award — Lunch Box (Sean Billings & Romeo Okwara) — $800
Jury Grand Prize — La Liga (Paul Rosenfeld & MacPherson Christopher) — $1,000
Pitch Contest Awards:
Jury Prize — Jürgen Locadia (Nic Chang) — $500
Audience Award — Jürgen Locadia (Nic Chang)
Additional Highlights:
SNY Official Selections: Lunch Box, Spit Out Your Bones, The Itinerant (to be featured in a television special)
Sports Documentary Course Audience Award — Jürgen Locadia (Nic Chang)
Inaugural Photography Competition Winner — Nicolás Galvis (Fight Night)
$500 prize + MLS media credential opportunity

Summary
With a packed opening night, strong panel attendance, international film participation, and a highly engaged audience throughout the weekend, the 2026 NYU Sports Film Festival marked a significant step forward in both scale and impact.
The festival continues to solidify its position as a leading platform for emerging sports storytellers and a growing hub for the intersection of sports, film, and culture.
